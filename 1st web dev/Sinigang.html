Sinigang History

Sinigang is a sour soup that originated in the Philippines before Spanish colonization. It's a staple of Filipino cuisine and is known for its unique flavor profile.

Sinigang:
Type: Soup or stew
Course: Main course
Place of origin: Philippines
Region or state: Tagalog Region

Ingredients:
2 pieces pork ribs/belly
2 to 3 cups kangkong leaves
3 pieces long green chili pepper (siling pansigang)
2 pieces medium tomato (quartered)
1 cup sliced daikon radish (Labanos)
10 to 12 pieces snake beans (sitaw), cut in 3-inch lengths (optional)
15 pieces okra
2 pieces taro (gabi), peeled and cut in half
8 to 10 cups water
2-3 packs of Knorr sinigang mix
3 tablespoons fish sauce (optional)
1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

Instructions:
1. Boil water in a pot.
2. Add onion and tomato. Boil for 5 to 8 minutes.
3. Put the pork in the cooking pot. Boil for 25 minutes.
4. Add the taro. Boil for 30 minutes.
5. Add the sinigang mix and ground black pepper. Stir. Add the fish sauce (optional).
6. Add the radish. Boil for 5 minutes.
7. Add the chili peppers, okra (and snake beans, if using). Let it stay covered for 5 minutes.
8. Add the kangkong leaves. Cover the pot and turn off the heat. Let it stay for 5 minutes.
9. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
